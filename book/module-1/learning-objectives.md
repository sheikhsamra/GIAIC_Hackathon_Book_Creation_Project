# Learning Objectives - Introduction to Physical AI

## Module Overview
This module introduces the fundamental concepts of Physical AI, distinguishing it from traditional AI and establishing the foundation for the rest of the textbook.

## Specific Learning Objectives

After completing this module, students will be able to:

### Knowledge Objectives
- **LO-M1-K1**: Define Physical AI and explain how it differs from traditional AI systems
- **LO-M1-K2**: Identify the core components that make up a Physical AI system
- **LO-M1-K3**: Describe the relationship between Physical AI and robotics
- **LO-M1-K4**: List key applications and use cases of Physical AI
- **LO-M1-K5**: Explain the main challenges in Physical AI development

### Comprehension Objectives
- **LO-M1-C1**: Distinguish between digital AI and embodied AI systems
- **LO-M1-C2**: Understand the importance of perception-action loops in Physical AI
- **LO-M1-C3**: Recognize the interdisciplinary nature of Physical AI (robotics, computer vision, ML, control theory)
- **LO-M1-C4**: Comprehend the role of simulation in Physical AI development
- **LO-M1-C5**: Understand safety considerations in Physical AI systems

### Application Objectives
- **LO-M1-A1**: Apply the Physical AI framework to analyze a simple robotic system
- **LO-M1-A2**: Identify Physical AI components in a given scenario
- **LO-M1-A3**: Evaluate the suitability of Physical AI vs. traditional AI for specific applications
- **LO-M1-A4**: Design a basic Physical AI system architecture for a simple task

### Analysis Objectives
- **LO-M1-An1**: Compare different approaches to implementing Physical AI systems
- **LO-M1-An2**: Analyze the trade-offs between simulation and real-world testing
- **LO-M1-An3**: Evaluate the safety implications of Physical AI system designs

## Assessment Methods

Students will demonstrate achievement of these objectives through:

- **Knowledge Checks**: Short quizzes covering key concepts
- **Discussion Questions**: Analytical questions about Physical AI applications
- **Simple Design Exercise**: Creating a basic Physical AI system architecture
- **Case Study Analysis**: Evaluating existing Physical AI implementations

## Prerequisites

Before starting this module, students should have:

- Basic understanding of artificial intelligence concepts
- Familiarity with programming concepts
- Understanding of basic mathematics (algebra, geometry)
- Basic knowledge of robotics (helpful but not required)

## Estimated Completion Time

- Reading and understanding content: 2-3 hours
- Completing exercises: 1-2 hours
- Taking quizzes: 30-45 minutes
- Total: 3.5-5.75 hours

## Success Criteria

Students will have successfully completed this module when they can:

- Accurately define Physical AI and distinguish it from traditional AI
- Explain the key components of Physical AI systems
- Identify appropriate applications for Physical AI
- Demonstrate understanding of safety considerations in Physical AI
- Apply Physical AI concepts to simple scenarios
